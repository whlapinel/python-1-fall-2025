Welcome!

About this course

This course is a 16-week, semester-long introduction to Python. Our main objective will be passing the Python Institute’s Certified Entry-Level Programmer (PCEP) certification.

We’ll take the PCEP exam soon after midterms. You’ll have 2 attempts, one on October 14th and another on October 17th.

Devices

You’ll be using the laptops I have in class. Personal devices are currently not authorized for use in class. You may use your personal devices for classwork outside of class, however.

AI

I strongly encourage you to use AI with caution. It is all too easy to rely on AI too heavily, become dependent on it, and find yourself in a bad situation. Use it for learning.

Assignments, Quizzes and Tests

  • Assignments, quizzes and tests may include:
    • Your work in the PE 1 course
    • Other programming assignments that I will provide to you along the way
    • Paper and online quizzes
    • Paper and online tests

Classroom rules

  1. No food or drink

  2. Treat devices with care and use responsibly

  3. No phones

  4. No off-task activity until your work is done

  5. Pay attention during lecture/discussion

    • Remove headphones, look up, listen, and be ready to be called upon. In return I will do my best to keep it short and interesting.

  6. Learn by doing

    • You simply must experiment with programming in order to learn it.

  7. Bathroom

    • One at a time, and if there’s no one already out then you may simply let me know you’re going.
